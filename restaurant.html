Fifteen years on Witherspoon.

The Owner

Michele Moriello has owned La Mezzaluna since 2010. The menu is his — Italian, north-leaning, particular about ingredients. The room runs small on purpose.

The Room

A long banquette along one wall. Wood booths along the other. White tablecloths. A single red flower on each table. The far wall holds a mural rendered in cream and beige — a slow curl of light that reads, depending on the light, as a half-moon or a wave.

The Food

The kitchen leans toward Northern Italy. Risotto in three preparations, including one built vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free for guests who need it. Truffle gnocchi when the season allows. Branzino, ossobuco, rack of lamb encrusted with hazelnut and walnut. Pappardelle al pesto, with smoked salmon, has its own following. The menu rotates with the season.

Gluten-free and whole-wheat pasta on request. Dietary accommodations are a conversation, not a footnote — ask your server.

The Name

Mezzaluna means half-moon. It is also the name of the curved, two-handled Italian knife used to mince herbs and slice pizza al taglio. The word holds both meanings simultaneously, the way the room does — a small celestial idea up on the mural and a working tool down in the kitchen. We have always meant both.

Partners

During the pandemic, La Mezzaluna cooked for hospitals, essential workers, and families in need. Still partnered with these organizations.
